About

I am a PhD Candidate whose research interest includes natural ecosystem modeling using remote sensing technologies and GIS. Check out my awesome work.

My PhD focus area is "Characterization of microtopography and its impact on carbon dynamics in tidal freshwater wetlands". I am working with US Forest Services and USDA in Santee Experimental Forest in South Carolina. I am using terrestrial liDAR, aerial LiDAR and UAS to characterize the microtopography and understanding its impact on C dynamics through biogeochemical modeling

Projects

I worked with NCDOT project "A deep learning based artificial intelligence approach for the automated classification of hydraulic structures". My role was to collect field data using unmanned aerial systems (UAS), Sonar, total station and RTK-GPS. I also carried out the GIS and photogrammetric analysis of field data.

I also worked in another NCDOT project "Geo-FRIT: A web-based geospatial analytics tool for quantifying freight risk and resilience in transportation" in which I conducted the literature review analyzing risk parameters due to different hazardous events and also carried out GIS analysis of traffic accidents in North Carolina.

I worked with Gaston County "Healthy Wells" project where I carried out the analytics of contaminants in well water and visualized it using GIS
